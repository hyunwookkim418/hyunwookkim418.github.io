CCTV Intermittent / No Video Issue (Switch Port Failure)

Last updated: 4/16/26

Problem Summary

One CCTV camera intermittently lost video feed, repeatedly disconnecting and reconnecting. Eventually, the video stopped displaying completely. Other cameras on the same network were operating normally, including those located farther away.

Why it matters?

  • Intermittent failure is harder to detect and diagnose than a full outage
  • High risk of misdiagnosis involving distance, cabling, or camera hardware

Environment

  • CCTV System: LTS IP Cameras
  • Network: PoE Switch (LTS)

Root Cause

  • Packet loss at the port level
  • Abnormally high latency
  • Link instability resulting in intermittent camera disconnection

Lessons Learned

  • Intermittent issues often indicate hardware degradation rather than configuration problems
  • Port-level faults can mimic cable or endpoint issues

Troubleshooting Process

1

Symptom Observation

  • CCTV feed repeatedly dropped and reconnected
  • Eventually no video output
  • Other cameras working normally
2

Initial Assumption Check

  • Distance issue? X (farther cameras working fine)
  • Camera issue? X (not yet confirmed)
  • Network instability suspected
3

Network Test (Key Evidence)

  • Ping test to camera IP showed packet loss
  • Response time was extremely slow
Result: Indicated a network-level issue rather than a distance or camera problem.
4

Isolation Test (Turning Point)

  • Moved the Ethernet cable to a different switch port
Result: Stable connectivity, no packet loss, and full video restoration.
